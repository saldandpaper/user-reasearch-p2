User Research Phase 2

1. Data Summary

1.1 Response Rate:

For this research, the team invited approximately 35 participants through various platforms, including Discord, social media, and personal outreach. The goal was to engage a broad audience of YouTube users who could provide relevant insights on their experiences and interest in an audio-only feature.

Total Responses Received:

A total of 20 responses were collected through the survey. This included participants who provided detailed feedback on their YouTube usage habits, preferences for video versus audio, and interest in an audio-only feature.

Response Rate Calculation:

Using the formula: Response Rate = (Total Responses/Total Invitation Sent) x 100

Response Rate = (20/50) x 100 = 57%

A 57% response rate reflects moderate engagement and interest from the targeted participant pool, suggesting that the outreach methods were effective in reaching individuals interested in YouTube’s functionalities.

1.2 Total Responses:

Total Surveys Completed:

20 participants completed the survey, providing insights into their YouTube usage habits, interest in an audio-only feature, and feedback on video vs. audio preferences.

Total Interviews Conducted:

In addition to the survey, 4 in-depth interviews were conducted to gather qualitative insights. These interviews allowed for a more detailed exploration of user behaviours and preferences that may not be fully captured in the survey.

Response Distribution:

The data collection consisted primarily of survey responses, with a smaller sample of in-depth interviews to support and validate the quantitative findings. This balanced approach provided both a broad overview of user preferences and deeper insights into specific user experiences.

1.4 Completion Time (Average):

Survey Completion Time:

Based on the collected data, the average time taken to complete the survey was approximately 5-7 minutes. Most participants provided concise but thoughtful responses, allowing for efficient data collection without compromising on the depth of insight.

Interview Duration:

The in-depth interviews lasted between 15-20 minutes, with an average duration of 18 minutes. This duration was sufficient to gather detailed feedback on participants’ YouTube habits and their interest in an audio-only feature. The interview format allowed for more nuanced responses, enhancing the understanding of user preferences and challenges.

1.5 Data Quality:

Survey Data Quality:

The survey responses were generally detailed and relevant, with most participants providing thoughtful answers to each question. This indicates that respondents took the time to consider their responses, contributing to high-quality data. The survey responses included a mix of quantitative and qualitative insights, with specific comments about their YouTube usage habits and interest in an audio-only feature. Some participants also offered additional suggestions, enriching the data with user perspectives on YouTube’s current functionalities.

Interview Data Quality:

The in-depth interviews yielded valuable insights that went beyond the survey data. Participants provided detailed accounts of their YouTube usage patterns, the impact of internet speed on their experience, and specific scenarios where an audio-only feature would be beneficial. This allowed for a deeper understanding of user needs and preferences. The interview data was especially useful for identifying pain points, such as data consumption concerns and the need for uninterrupted audio playback.

Consistency Across Responses:

Overall, there was consistency in responses, with common themes emerging around data-saving benefits, battery efficiency, and the flexibility to listen without video. However, a few participants mentioned they primarily use YouTube for visual content, showing a range of preferences.

Challenges in Data Quality:

A small portion of survey responses were brief or vague, indicating that some participants may not have fully engaged with every question. This did not significantly impact the overall findings but is noted as a minor limitation.

In summary, the data collected is of high quality, providing both broad and in-depth insights into user interest in an audio-only YouTube feature. The mix of quantitative survey results and qualitative interview feedback strengthens the reliability of the findings.

3. Reflection on Data Collection Experience

In this phase, our team implemented the UX research plan by conducting surveys and interviews to gather insights into YouTube users’ interest in an audio-only feature. This reflection outlines our experience with data collection, discussing successes, challenges, and potential improvements for future research.

3.1 Expected vs. Actual Process

Initial Expectations:

We anticipated collecting responses primarily from frequent YouTube users who would have strong opinions on the platform’s features. Based on our outreach strategy, we expected a high engagement rate, particularly in Discord communities and among social media groups interested in music and streaming.

Actual Experience:

Overall, the data collection process went as planned, with 20 survey responses and a few in-depth interviews completed. The response rate was close to our expectations, though engagement in some communities was lower than anticipated. Participants generally provided detailed answers, especially in the interview sessions, where they elaborated on their use of YouTube and potential benefits of an audio-only feature.

3.2 Challenges Encountered

Recruitment Challenges:

Finding participants outside our personal networks proved challenging, as some online communities showed limited engagement. Despite our outreach efforts in Discord and social media, participation was not as high in certain groups, possibly due to the niche nature of the topic.

Data Quality Variability:

While most responses were insightful, a few survey responses lacked depth, with some participants providing brief or vague answers. This was particularly evident in questions about additional suggestions, where some responses were minimal.

Technical and Logistical Issues:

Scheduling interviews was sometimes difficult, as participants had varying availability. Additionally, coordinating within our team to manage data collection and maintain consistency in interviews required careful planning.

3.3 Improvements for Future Data Collection

Enhanced Recruitment Strategy:

For future studies, we would expand recruitment to include more diverse online platforms and explore offering incentives to encourage participation. Broadening the outreach to other social media channels and potentially targeting YouTube-specific forums could attract a more engaged participant pool.

Simplifying Survey Questions:

To ensure high-quality responses, we would consider refining and clarifying survey questions further. For example, rephrasing questions to be more straightforward may improve response depth, especially for open-ended sections.

Structured Interview Guide:

During the interviews, a more structured guide could help streamline responses and ensure we capture key insights efficiently. This would also reduce the variability in responses and focus on specific areas relevant to our research questions.

In summary, the data collection phase was successful, though it revealed some areas for improvement. By refining our recruitment approach, question clarity, and interview process, we could enhance data quality and ensure even more meaningful insights in future phases.

4. Conclusion

This phase of our research provided valuable insights into YouTube users’ habits and their interest in an audio-only feature. Through surveys and in-depth interviews, we identified key trends, including the high frequency of YouTube usage and significant interest in an audio-focused experience. Our findings show that many users already use YouTube as an audio-only platform, reinforcing the demand for a feature that allows audio playback without video streaming. Additionally, the data highlighted specific benefits users seek, such as reduced data consumption, extended battery life, and uninterrupted audio streaming.

Despite some challenges in participant recruitment and data quality variability, our overall data collection process was effective. The feedback gathered not only validates the potential appeal of an audio-only option but also provides YouTube with actionable insights on user preferences and potential areas for enhancement.

Moving forward, the insights from this phase will guide us in refining our recommendations and developing a prototype or concept for an audio-only feature. By addressing the identified challenges and implementing the suggested improvements, we can enhance future research phases and continue building a comprehensive case for introducing this feature on YouTube.
